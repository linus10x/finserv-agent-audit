## Permitted uses

The following uses are permitted without separate license, subject to the conditions stated:

**P1. Educational citation.** Academic publications, blog posts, conference talks, podcast appearances, regulator-facing white papers, and similar educational uses may cite the mark with appropriate designation (™ or ®). Example permitted use: *"The Autonomy Ladder™ framework (Bhaduri, 2026) describes a five-level classification A0 through A4 for autonomous-agent governance in regulated environments."*

**P2. "Built on Autonomy Ladder™" attribution badge.** Adopters of the framework may use the badge "Built on Autonomy Ladder™" (or the equivalent badge image, to be provided by the trademark holder) in their product documentation, README files, system-design documents, and similar adopter-facing surfaces. The badge attribution must not imply endorsement of the adopter's specific deployment by the trademark holder. Example permitted use: *"Our autonomous-agent platform is built on Autonomy Ladder™ governance patterns (MIT license; see github.com/linus10x/finserv-agent-audit)."*

**P3. Internal-document use.** Adopters' internal compliance documentation, internal training materials, internal governance committee minutes, and similar internal-only documents may use the mark without separate license. The "internal-only" carve-out covers documents that are not distributed beyond the adopter's named organization.

**P4. Regulator citation.** Adopters and their counsel may cite the mark in regulator submissions, examination responses, and supervisory correspondence without separate license. The trademark holder considers regulator-facing citation a permitted use in every jurisdiction where the mark is asserted.

**P5. Press and analyst coverage.** Press, industry analysts, and trade publications may use the mark in their coverage without separate license. The trademark holder requests (but does not require) advance notice for advance press coverage so accuracy review can be offered.

## Prohibited uses

The following uses are prohibited without separate written license from the trademark holder:

**X1. Implied endorsement.** No use of the mark may imply that the trademark holder has endorsed, certified, approved, or otherwise reviewed the user's specific product, service, or deployment. Example prohibited use: *"Autonomy Ladder™-approved [Vendor X] AI platform."* Permitted alternative: *"Built on Autonomy Ladder™ governance patterns — [Vendor X]'s implementation, not endorsed by the trademark holder."*

**X2. Derivative trademarks with "Ladder" suffix.** No party may register, apply to register, or use in commerce any mark consisting of "[Word] Ladder" in the autonomy / governance / AI / financial-services / commercial-real-estate / risk-management spaces, where "[Word]" is a substitute term that would create consumer-confusion likelihood with "Autonomy Ladder™". Examples of prohibited derivative marks: "Autonomy Ladders", "Compliance Ladder", "Governance Ladder", "AI Ladder", "Risk Ladder" in any of the named spaces.

**X3. Certification-mark-style implications.** No use of the mark may suggest that the user is certified, accredited, or otherwise recognized as competent by the trademark holder. The framework is open-source patterns; there is no certification scheme (and will not be one, except per the deferred ALO™ evaluation above, which would be a separate mark with separate license terms). Example prohibited use: *"[Person X] is an Autonomy Ladder™-certified consultant."* Permitted alternative: *"[Person X] consults on Autonomy Ladder™ adoption (no certification scheme exists; Person X's competence is asserted by Person X, not by the trademark holder)."*

**X4. Commercial-product naming.** No commercial product may be named "Autonomy Ladder" or any confusingly-similar variant (per X2 above). Adopters' products MAY reference the framework via the P2 "Built on" badge but MAY NOT use the mark as the primary product name.

**X5. Domain registration.** No party may register a domain name consisting of "autonomyladder" or any confusingly-similar variant in any TLD without prior written license. The trademark holder owns `autonomy-ladder.io` (primary surface) and `autonomy-ladder.com` (301 redirect to .io). Defensive registrations in additional TLDs are an ongoing trademark-holder workstream.

**X6. Trade-dress imitation.** The visual design system associated with the framework (color palette, typography, layout patterns, badge designs) is part of the trademark holder's trade dress. Imitation that would create likelihood of consumer confusion is prohibited even where the literal word mark is not used.

## Irrevocable nominative-use grants (modeled on the Linux Foundation, Kubernetes, and Rust Foundation marks)

The following grants are **irrevocable** for the recipients identified, are extended without separate license or notice, and survive any future change of trademark ownership or any future amendment to this document. They are made to pre-empt the perception that trademark registration of a discipline-vocabulary mark over a permissively-licensed OSS project would chill legitimate downstream use; that perception is not warranted, and these grants document that fact in writing.

**N1. OSS forks of this repository.** Any fork of `linus10x/finserv-agent-audit` (and the sibling `linus10x/cre-agent-audit`) may use the mark in the fork's repository name, documentation, source files, and README solely for the purpose of identifying the fork's origin and the patterns it derives from. The fork must not assert origin from the trademark holder beyond the fact of derivation, must not use the mark in a manner that would create likelihood of confusion as to source or sponsorship, and must include a clear "Fork of `linus10x/finserv-agent-audit`" or equivalent statement of derivation in the README.

**N2. Conformance attestation.** Any adopter of the framework may publicly state that their system "implements the Autonomy Ladder™ A0-A4 framework" or "conforms to the Autonomy Ladder™ governance patterns" without separate license, provided the statement is truthful (i.e., the system actually implements the cited surface). Adopters may NOT claim certification, accreditation, or endorsement by the trademark holder absent a separate certification license.

**N3. Academic citation and scholarly publication.** Cited use of the mark in academic papers, dissertations, conference proceedings, technical reports, working papers, books, book chapters, blog posts of substantively-scholarly character, and presentations is permitted without separate license. This grant covers comparative analysis, criticism, hypothesis testing against the framework, replication studies, and any pedagogical use including reference in syllabi and instructional materials.

**N4. Standards-development and regulatory-process use.** Use of the mark by standards bodies (LF AI & Data Foundation, OpenSSF, IEEE, ISO/IEC committees, NIST working groups, FFIEC working groups, supervisory authorities), regulators (federal banking agencies, state insurance commissioners, securities regulators, EU national competent authorities under the EU AI Act, the UK FCA, MAS Singapore), and counsel acting on behalf of any of the foregoing is permitted without separate license. This grant is unconditional and survives any change of ownership of the mark.

**N5. Comparator-set inclusion.** Inclusion of the mark in a list of frameworks alongside named comparators (NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, OWASP Top 10, MITRE ATT&CK, OpenSSF Best Practices, EU AI Act Annex III, SR 11-7, OCC 2011-12, OCC 2026-13, etc.) is permitted without separate license. The trademark holder welcomes inclusion of the Autonomy Ladder™ framework in industry comparator sets.

**Restrictions on the grants above.** N1-N5 do not authorize: (a) use of the mark to imply official certification or endorsement by the trademark holder (separate certification license required); (b) use of the mark in a manner that disparages or harms the reputation of the framework; (c) registration of confusingly-similar domain names (X5 prohibition retained); (d) trade-dress imitation (X6 prohibition retained); (e) use of the mark in a manner inconsistent with the Apache License 2.0 §6 trademark disclaimer governing source-code use of files licensed under the Apache arm of the dual license.

**Reference precedents.** The grants in N1-N5 are modeled on:
- The Linux Foundation Trademark Usage Guidelines (`linuxfoundation.org/legal/trademark-usage`)
- The Cloud Native Computing Foundation Trademark Usage Guidelines (`cncf.io/about/trademark-usage`)
- The Kubernetes mark policy (`kubernetes.io/community/trademarks`)
- The Rust Foundation Trademark Policy (`foundation.rust-lang.org/policies/trademark-policy-draft`)

Adopters in doubt may rely on the most-permissive interpretation across the four precedents above; the trademark holder confirms that intent at the time of this document's publication.

## Fair-use carve-out

The following fair-use exceptions apply notwithstanding the prohibitions above:

**F1. Academic publication.** Academic publications may use the mark to refer to the framework as the subject of scholarly analysis, criticism, or comparison without separate license. Example: *"This paper compares the Autonomy Ladder™ framework's A2 boundary to the European AI Act's risk-tier structure..."*

**F2. Press, criticism, commentary.** Press coverage, critical commentary, comparative analysis, and editorial commentary may use the mark without separate license. The trademark holder does not assert trademark rights against legitimate journalistic, critical, or editorial use.

**F3. Regulator citation.** As stated in P4 above, regulator-facing use is permitted without separate license. Counsel acting on behalf of an adopter or a regulator may cite the mark in any official capacity without separate license.

**F4. Nominative fair use.** Use of the mark to refer to the framework where no other reasonable identifier exists is permitted (nominative fair use). Example: *"We evaluated the OWASP Top 10, the MITRE ATT&CK framework, and the Autonomy Ladder™ governance patterns..."* — nominative use to refer to the framework as one item in a list of comparator frameworks.
